Why I do this

I taught myself game development with slow internet and no industry around me. It shouldn't have to be that hard. So I'm working on Myanmar's game industry - teaching developers in our own language, publishing their games to the world, and trying to prove, project by project, that world-class games can come from anywhere.

Roadmap

One mission, two companies. Each phase pays for the next.

  1. Educate - now

    Train the talent the industry doesn't have yet

    Game development education in Burmese through GMM's academy - 100+ developers so far, with free publishing for students who finish what they start.

  2. Publish - now

    Put Myanmar-made games on world storefronts

    Five titles published as founder - from Witching Nights: Tower Defense on Google Play to Anominal and Them's Tale on Steam. Each one is proof that local developers can ship to a global standard.

  3. Prove - next

    Make engagement an engineering discipline

    Through NPI: serious games and gamification that move measurable business KPIs - the service engine that funds original creative work without surrendering it.

  4. Elevate - beyond

    Make Myanmar a serious player in the global game industry

    Original titles on global platforms, a publishing bridge international studios trust, and a generation of developers who never had to teach themselves alone.
