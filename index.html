Why I do this

I'm a gamer first - I fell in love with games long before I ever made one. Then I taught myself to build them with slow internet and no industry around me. It shouldn't have to be that hard. So I see chaos and create structure:

  • Teach - game development in our own language, so the next generation starts years ahead of where I did.
  • Develop - games that entertain, and games that solve real problems for organizations.
  • Publish - Myanmar-made games on world storefronts, with developer-first terms.
  • Prove - project by project, that world-class games can come from anywhere.

Roadmap

One mission, two companies. Each phase pays for the next.

  1. Teach - now

    Train the talent the industry doesn't have yet

    Game development education in Burmese through GMM's academy - 200+ developers and counting, with free publishing for students who finish what they start.

  2. Develop - now

    Build games that entertain, and games that matter

    The craft behind both ventures: entertainment titles I ship as a developer, and serious, impactful games that solve real problems for organizations.

  3. Publish - now

    Put Myanmar-made games on world storefronts

    Titles published as founder - from Witching Nights: Tower Defense on Google Play to Them's Tale on Steam. Each one is proof that local developers can ship to a global standard.

  4. Prove - next

    Make engagement an engineering discipline

    Through NagaPlus: serious games and gamification that move measurable business KPIs - the engine that funds original creative work without surrendering it.

  5. Elevate - beyond

    Make Myanmar a serious player in global games

    Original titles on global platforms, a publishing bridge international studios trust, and a generation of developers who never had to teach themselves alone.
